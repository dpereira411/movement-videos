Calf Straight

60r

  1. Keep your weight entered on the ball of your foot during the calf raises
  2. If centered correctly, you should be able to lift your little toe off the ground

Elevated Seiza

120s

  1. Any elevated surface will do; for example a rolled up bath towel
  2. The ankles must be completely extended before beginning to time the set

Calf Outward

60r

  1. Turn the toes outward as much as you are comfortable
  2. Keep the knees locked at all times

Squatted Seiza

120s

  1. Feel the front of the ankles pressing forward for the duration of the set
  2. The higher the knees the greater the intensity of the stretch

Calf Inward

60r

  1. Keep the toes together, turn the heels outward as far as possible
  2. Keep the heels wide at all times; note that they will have tendency to drift inward during the set if you allow it

Curled Seiza

120s

  1. Curl the toes under the foot as much as possible; bend those toes!
  2. Leaning forward will help to decrease the intensity of the set if necessary; especially initially

2 Pike 2 Squat

10r

  1. Bend the knees fully open entry into each squat
  2. The knees should be completely locked during the pike bounces

Standing Pike

120s

  1. Allow the torso to hand freely during the pike
  2. Cross the arms at the elbow if your pike is sufficiently deep to warrant it

2 Pike 2 Squat

10r

  1. Pull the chest deeply to the knees during the pike bounces
  2. Press the heels as deeply as possible to the floor during the squat bounces

Kneeling Achiles R

90s

  1. The rear knee should be pulled out to the side to remove any hip flexor stretch
  2. The heel, toe and knee of the front leg should all create a single forward line

Kneeling Achiles L

90s

  1. Lean forward strongly, creating as much flexion in the front ankle as possible
  2. Keel the heel of the front foot pressed firmly into the ground/li>

Standing Achilles on Block R

90s

  1. Use a stretch block or other stable surface to elevate the front of the foot
  2. Keep the heel pressed down at all times

Standing Achilles on Block L

90s

  1. You may find that placing both hands just above the front knee and gently pressing backward will greatly increase the intensity of your stretch

Narrow Hurdler Block R

90s

  1. If you are particularly tight, you may find it helpful to sit the right glute up on a pillow or stretch block
  2. Keep the knee of the bent leg pressed firmly into the knee of the straight leg for this variation

Narrow Hurdler Block L

90s

  1. A stretch strap wrapped around the front foot works wonders to accelerate your progress

Wide Hurdler Block R

90s

  1. Pull the bend knee as far as to the rear as possible
  2. While doing so, keep the foot pulled tight into the glutes

Wide Hurdler Block L

90s

  1. Do not simply hunch over, stretch the chin forward towards the toes

Standing Single Pike R

90s

  1. Place the shin of the bent leg vertically on the wall
  2. This is a very intense stretch, feel free to place your hands on top of two stretch blocks as necessary to decrease the intensity to a manageable level

Standing Single Pike L

90s

  1. Keep the standing leg completely straight

Vertical Lunge Bent R

90s

  1. Keep the rear knee and shin pressed firmly into the wall
  2. Performed perfectly, the rear quad should be completely flat on the ground at the deepest point of this stretch

Vertical Lunge Straight R

90s

  1. Use stretch blocks as necessary to control the depth of this stretch

Vertical Lunge Straight L

90s

  1. Do not settle for having the front leg straight, get that rear quad down flat on the ground

Standing Pike

120s

  1. Use stretch blocks as necessary to control the intensity and depth of this stretch
  2. If the chest is not on the knees, the pike still has room for improvement
